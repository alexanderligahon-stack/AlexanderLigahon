School Projects

I created a 5-part YouTube Shorts series called “Secrets Movies Use to Make You Feel Emotions,” where each video explores a different way filmmakers manipulate our feelings. Part 1 focused on music, sound, and camera angles, showing how the right soundtrack or perspective can instantly make you feel tension, joy, or sadness. Part 2 explored editing and pacing, highlighting how fast cuts, slow moments, and perfectly timed reveals control your emotional response. Part 3 delved into acting and facial expressions, revealing how micro-expressions, body language, and eye contact communicate emotions without words. Part 4 centered on storytelling and plot twists, explaining how emotional arcs and relatable characters keep viewers engaged, and Part 5 wrapped up with visual effects and sound design, demonstrating how CGI, effects, and sound layering amplify every emotional beat on screen. Together, these shorts offer a compact and engaging look at the hidden techniques behind movies that make audiences feel so strongly.

Creating this series taught me a lot about both content and technical skills. I wrote scripts that were short, punchy, and easy to follow, ideal for 60-second Shorts. I learned how to plan visuals to match narration, choosing stock footage, images, and text animations that illustrate each point without ever showing my face. I also experimented with voice recording and AI voice tools, discovering how to make my narration sound clear and engaging, even when using a neutral, faceless style. Every part of the series pushed me to think creatively about pacing, timing, and visual storytelling, which are skills I had to teach myself step by step.

Even though my first attempts weren’t perfect, this project showed me that self-teaching and experimentation can produce meaningful results. I learned video editing in CapCut, adjusting animations, transitions, and text overlays to make the content visually engaging. I figured out how to sync voiceovers with visuals, add subtle motion to static images, and enhance scenes with music and sound effects. By producing all five Shorts on my own, I not only improved my editing skills but also built confidence in planning, scripting, and creating content from scratch. It was a challenging but rewarding process that proved even imperfect learning can lead to professional-looking results.
